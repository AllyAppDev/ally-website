DMCA & Copyright Policy

Last updated: July 2026 ~6 min read

We respect intellectual property rights. This page explains how to report copyright infringement on Ally and what happens next — including how to contest a takedown if you've been falsely targeted.

1 Our Respect for Intellectual Property

Ally is a platform for people to communicate and build communities. We believe strongly in both free expression and intellectual property rights. These two things don't have to conflict — and we work hard to uphold both.

Under the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) and similar international laws, Ally operates as an online service provider. This means we can take down infringing content when properly notified, and our users can contest wrongful takedowns through the counter-notification process.

Our Terms of Service require users to respect copyright. Posting content you don't own or have permission to share is a violation of your agreement with Ally.

2 Filing a DMCA Takedown Notice

If you believe that content on Ally infringes your copyright, you can submit a DMCA takedown notice to our designated agent. To be valid and actionable, your notice must include:

  • 1Your full legal name and contact information (email, mailing address, phone number).
  • 2A description of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed. If multiple works are covered by a single notice, provide a representative list.
  • 3The URL or specific location on Ally where the infringing content can be found. Be as specific as possible — vague descriptions ("content on Ally") won't allow us to identify what to remove.
  • 4A statement that you have a good faith belief that the use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  • 5A statement, made under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or are authorized to act on the owner's behalf.
  • 6Your physical or electronic signature.

⚠️ Submitting a DMCA notice with materially false information is a violation of law. If you're not sure content infringes your copyright, consult a lawyer before filing.

3 Counter-Notification

If your content was removed due to a DMCA notice and you believe the removal was incorrect — for example, because you have a license to use the content, the content is in the public domain, or the use qualifies as fair use — you may file a counter-notification.

Your counter-notification must include:

  • 1Your full legal name and contact information (email, mailing address, phone number).
  • 2Identification of the content that was removed and its location before removal.
  • 3A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief that the content was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.
  • 4A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal district court for your location (or, if outside the US, any judicial district in which Ally may be found).
  • 5Your physical or electronic signature.

Send your counter-notification to dmca@allyapp.cc with subject line: DMCA Counter-Notification — [content description].

What happens next

When we receive a valid counter-notification, we'll forward it to the original complainant. If the complainant does not notify us within 10–14 business days that they've filed a lawsuit seeking a court order to prevent the restoration of the content, we may restore the content.

✅ We take counter-notifications seriously. If you've been wrongly targeted, we want to make it right.

4 Repeat Infringer Policy

In accordance with the DMCA, Ally maintains a policy for terminating accounts of users who repeatedly infringe copyright.

Here's how our three-strike system works:

  • First strike: Content removed. Warning issued to your account. Educational information provided about copyright.
  • Second strike: Content removed. Formal warning. Temporary restrictions may be applied to upload capabilities.
  • Third strike: Account terminated. This follows from a pattern of confirmed copyright infringement.

A "strike" is recorded when a valid DMCA notice is received and processed, or when we independently identify clear copyright infringement. Strikes may expire after 12 months without incident, at our discretion.

Counter-notifications that result in content being restored do not count as strikes.

⚠️ We also reserve the right to terminate accounts immediately in cases of flagrant, willful copyright infringement (e.g., operating a server explicitly dedicated to distributing copyrighted material without authorization).
