Community Guidelines

Last updated: July 2026 ~8 min read

These guidelines are about the kind of community we want to build together. They go beyond what's technically prohibited — they're about how we treat each other and what makes Ally genuinely great.

1 Our Values

Ally is built on three core values that shape everything from our product decisions to how we moderate the platform. These aren't marketing words — they're commitments we take seriously.

🤝

Respect

Everyone deserves to feel welcome. We treat each other with basic dignity, even when we disagree.

🔒

Privacy

We protect the privacy of every person on the platform — not just legally, but as a genuine value.

🛡️

Safety

Ally should be safe for everyone — especially people in vulnerable groups or situations.

2 Be Kind

This one sounds obvious, but it makes a real difference. Ally is full of people from different backgrounds, cultures, and life experiences. What's normal in one community might be unusual in another — and that diversity is a strength.

💬

Disagree constructively

Debate ideas, not people. It's completely fine to strongly disagree with someone — just focus on what they said, not who they are.

❤️

Welcome newcomers

Everyone's new somewhere. If someone asks a basic question or makes a beginner mistake, help them out instead of piling on.

🌍

Be mindful of context

Humor, sarcasm, and in-jokes that work in one group can land badly in another. Read the room, and be kind when pointing out misunderstandings.

🚫

Don't harass or bully

Targeted harassment — repeatedly going after someone, coordinating others to pile on, or making someone feel unsafe — is not allowed. This includes DMs.

3 Keep It Safe

Safety means different things in different contexts — physical safety, emotional safety, and online safety all matter on Ally.

🏥

Take mental health seriously

Don't trivialize self-harm or suicide. If someone shares that they're struggling, treat it with care. Resources like the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (US) or Crisis Text Line exist for a reason — it's okay to share them.

🔞

Age-appropriate content

Adult content is allowed only in servers where the server admin has enabled it in age-gated channels. Never share adult content in spaces accessible to minors. Never share sexual content involving minors — this is a zero-tolerance rule.

💊

No dangerous promotion

Don't promote dangerous behaviors (self-harm methods, unsafe drug combinations) in ways that could encourage others to hurt themselves. Harm reduction information shared responsibly is different from promotion.

🎯

No hate speech

Content that dehumanizes people based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected characteristics isn't allowed. Critique ideas; don't dehumanize people.

4 Respect Privacy

Privacy is core to who we are as a company, and it has to be core to who we are as a community.

🔐

Keep private things private

If someone shares something in a private channel, DM, or closed server, it's understood to stay there. Don't screenshot and share conversations without consent.

📍

Don't share others' personal information

This includes real names, addresses, workplaces, phone numbers, and photos of someone who hasn't shared them publicly. Even information that's "technically" public can be weaponized — use judgment.

🤫

Respect anonymity

Some people participate under pseudonyms for very good reasons. Don't try to "reveal" who someone is unless they've offered that information themselves. Outing someone — whether identity, sexuality, or location — can cause real harm.

5 Server Rules & Admin Authority

Ally servers (communities) are run by server administrators who create their own cultures and rules. Server admins have broad authority to shape their communities — including setting their own rules, restricting topics, and banning members — as long as they stay within Ally's platform policies.

As a user:

  • Respect the rules of each server you join. Every server is different.
  • If you disagree with a server's rules, you're free to leave — and to appeal server bans through the server's own process or by contacting the admin.
  • Don't harass or attack server admins for doing their jobs. Moderation is a thankless task.

As a server admin:

  • You're responsible for ensuring your server complies with Ally's platform policies and AUP.
  • You have the tools to mute, kick, ban, and set permissions — use them fairly.
  • Severe violations you witness (CSAM, terrorism, etc.) should be reported to Ally Trust & Safety even if you've already removed the member from your server.

6 Content Warnings

Content warnings (CWs) are a community courtesy. They let people choose whether to engage with content that might be difficult for them — without hiding the content or judging it as "bad."

We encourage (but don't require) using content warnings for:

⚠️ Graphic violence ⚠️ Disturbing news ⚠️ Mental health topics ⚠️ Flashing/strobing images ⚠️ Spoilers ⚠️ Political content ⚠️ Food/diet topics

Server admins can make content warnings required in specific channels. Ally's spoiler functionality (wrapping text in spoiler tags) is a built-in way to implement this.

7 Enforcement & Appeals

Ally's Trust & Safety team handles platform-level enforcement. Server admins handle enforcement within their own servers.

Platform enforcement follows a progressive model: most issues get a warning first. Serious violations skip straight to suspension or permanent ban. See our Acceptable Use Policy for the full enforcement framework.

📬 Platform enforcement actions can be appealed within 30 days at appeals@allyapp.cc.

We try to be consistent and fair, but moderation is inherently human. If we get something wrong, tell us and we'll take another look.

8 How to Report

Reporting is one of the most important contributions a community member can make. Here's how:

🖱️

In-app report button

Right-click or long-press any message, right-click any profile, or use the server context menu to find the Report option. It takes less than a minute.

📧

Email for complex issues

For issues that need more context, email safety@allyapp.cc. Include usernames, server names, and timestamps if you have them.

🚨

Urgent safety matters

For active threats or CSAM, safety@allyapp.cc is monitored around the clock. Don't hesitate.

Your identity is protected when you report — we don't reveal reporters to the person being reported. Reports made in good faith, even if no violation is found, are always welcome.
